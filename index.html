Qemmet

Qemmet is a way to generate a Qiskit 0.3 or OpenQASM 3.0 code with a shorthand notation.

How does this work?

The syntax consists mainly of 3 parts:
quantum_register_num?<int>;classical_register_num?<int>;gate_string<string>

The gate_string is a string in the form of (c*?[zyxhmb][\d\s]*)+.

If you cannot read a regular expression, click here.

Go here: https://regex101.com/

Examples and outputs, in no particular order:
  • x: Apply an X gate to all quantum registers.
  • x12: Apply an X gate to quantum register 1 and 2.
  • cx: Apply a CX gate to quantum register 1 and 2, where 1 is the control and 2 is the target.
  • cx34: Apply a CX gate to quantum register 3 and 4, where 3 is the control and 4 is the target.
  • ccx123: Apply a CCX gate to quantum register 1, 2, and 3, where 1 and 2 are the controls and 3 is the target.
  • cccccx123456: Apply a CCCCCX gate to quantum register 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 where 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 are the controls and 6 is the target.
  • cccx12 1 11 9: Apply a CCCX gate to quantum register 12, 1, 11 and 9 where 12, 1, and 11 are the controls and 9 is the target. Please keep in mind that if you separate the numbers, you have to separate all of them. If not, it will be treated as a single digit packed together (and will get separated later digit by digit).
Example circuits:
  • 2;;h1cx12: Bell's state.
  • ;1;hm: 1-bit randomizer.
  • 4;3;x4hccx134h123x123ccz123x123h123ccx134h123x123ccz123x123h123m123: Grover's search.
  • 3;;h2cx23cxh1m12cx23cz13: Quantum teleportation.
Notes:
  • For ease of human counting, the register starts from 1, not 0.
  • Currently this is only parser not syntax checker, so please check the syntax again by yourself.
  • This is a prototype right now. It only supports x, y, z, Hadamard (h), Measurement (m), and Barrier (b).
  • The code itself is super spaghetti, but if you can stand them and want to add something just open a pull request!
